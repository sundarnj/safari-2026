Itinerary

6 Days Naivasha / Masai Mara Private Safari — Axis Africa Safaris

ThuAug 20

Day 1 · Arrival in Nairobi

All seven travelers land in Nairobi throughout the morning. Check in and rest — it's Day 1 of the adventure! That evening, we also celebrate Sundar & Sweta's 3rd wedding anniversary 🎉 with a special dinner.

🏨 Hyatt Place Nairobi Westlands
FriAug 21

Day 2 · Nairobi → Naivasha

Morning pick-up, brief safari orientation, then drive to Lake Naivasha via the breathtaking Great Rift Valley viewpoint (≈3 hrs). Afternoon sunset boat ride among hippos and a walk on Crescent Island.

🏨 Lake Naivasha Resort
SatAug 22

Day 3 · Naivasha → Masai Mara

After breakfast, a scenic 5-hour drive through rolling green plains into the Maasai Mara. Check into camp inside the Ol Kinyei Conservancy, then an afternoon game drive followed by a night game drive to spot nocturnal wildlife.

🏕️ Porini Mara Camp
SunAug 23

Day 4 · Game Drives at the Conservancy

A full day exploring Naboisho and Ol Kinyei Conservancy at your own pace. Game drives guaranteed throughout the day and evening — no crowds, pure wilderness.

🏕️ Porini Mara Camp
MonAug 24

Day 5 · Masai Mara Extended Game Drive

Early breakfast then a game drive en route to Ashnil Mara Camp. After check-in and lunch, an afternoon drive through the Masai Mara National Park hunting for the Big Five — lions, elephants, leopards, buffalo, and rhino.

🏕️ Ashnil Mara Camp
TueAug 25

Day 6 · 🎈 Hot Air Balloon + Full Day Drive

06:15 — Balloon takes off at dawn for a 1-hour flight over the Mara. Bird's-eye view of the Great Rift Valley, followed by a champagne breakfast in the bush.

Then a full day game drive through the heart of the Mara — vast herds of wildebeest and zebras, possible Mara River crossing (July–September season), picnic lunch on the savanna.

🏕️ Ashnil Mara Camp
WedAug 26

Day 7 · Maasai Village + Return to Nairobi

After breakfast, an immersive visit to a traditional Maasai village — vibrant traditions, intricate beadwork, dances and songs. Check out ≈9:00 am, then drive back to Nairobi. All flights depart Nairobi in the late evening.

Packing List

Guidance from our safari guide at Axis Africa Safaris. Click to expand each category.

  • Comfortable warm layers — mornings in an open vehicle can be cold
  • Light clothing: shorts, T-shirts, tank tops for midday heat
  • Hat or cap for sun (less essential inside the vehicle, but handy)
  • Light fleece or jacket for early morning drives
  • Neutral/earth tones preferred (avoid bright white)
  • Comfortable shoes for sitting in a vehicle and short walks
  • Thick soles recommended — acacia thorns on the ground
  • Sandals fine for camp; closed shoes better for bush walks
  • High-SPF sunscreen — you're sitting on the Equator
  • Sunglasses (wraparound style ideal)
  • After-sun lotion
  • Wet wipes — camps do not provide them
  • Mosquito repellent (DEET-based or equivalent)
  • Flashlight / torch — essential at Masai Mara camp where the generator turns off at night
  • Power bank — charge your devices during the day
  • UK plug adapter (Type G) — Kenya uses British 3-pin sockets, 220–240V / 50Hz
  • Camera or camcorder with a large memory card
  • Kenya SIM: Safaricom or Airtel available at the airport on arrival
  • Passport (valid for at least 6 months beyond Aug 2026)
  • Kenya ETA confirmation printout (where applicable)
  • Travel insurance documents
  • Flight confirmation printouts
  • Yellow Fever vaccination certificate if required by your origin country
  • Prescribed malaria prophylaxis (start before travel — see Health section)
  • Anti-diarrheal tablets (e.g. Imodium)
  • Oral rehydration salts
  • Antihistamines
  • Motion sickness tablets (long drives on dirt roads)
  • Personal prescription medications — bring extra supply
  • Pain relievers (paracetamol / ibuprofen)

Health Preparation

Consult a travel medicine clinic at least 6–8 weeks before departure for personalised advice.

💉

Recommended Vaccinations

  • Yellow Fever (required if arriving from endemic country)
  • Typhoid
  • Hepatitis A & B
  • Tetanus / Diphtheria
  • Rabies (optional — for extended bush time)
  • Routine vaccines up to date (MMR, flu)
🦟

Malaria Prophylaxis

  • Masai Mara is a malaria zone — prevention is important
  • Common options: Malarone (Atovaquone/Proguanil) or Doxycycline
  • Start Malarone 1–2 days before travel; Doxycycline 1–2 days before
  • August is dry season — lower risk, but don't skip it
  • Use mosquito repellent every evening
🧳

Medicines to Pack

  • Anti-diarrheal (Imodium)
  • Oral rehydration salts
  • Antihistamines
  • Motion sickness tablets
  • Pain relievers
  • All personal prescriptions + extra supply

Weather in Kenya — August

We're going at the best possible time. August is peak dry season and the height of the Great Migration.

🌡️

Temperature

15–25°C (59–77°F) in the Mara. Warm midday, cool mornings and evenings — bring layers for the open vehicle.

☀️

Dry Season

Very little rain. Excellent visibility. Wildlife concentrates around rivers and water sources, making sightings easier.

🦓

Great Migration

July–September is prime wildebeest migration season. We have a real chance of watching the dramatic Mara River crossings.

🎈

Perfect Balloon Weather

Calm, clear August mornings are ideal for hot air ballooning — our Day 6 sunrise flight couldn't be better timed.

Meet the Big Five

The Masai Mara is one of the best places on Earth to see all five. Keep your eyes open.

🦁

Lion

Apex predator. Usually found resting in the shade or on rocky outcrops. The Masai Mara has one of Africa's densest lion populations.

Common sighting
🐘

Elephant

The largest land animal on Earth. Families roam the conservancies daily, led by a matriarch with decades of wisdom.

Common sighting
🐆

Leopard

The elusive one. Nocturnal and solitary, often spotted draped in the branches of trees. Your night drives improve the odds considerably.

Elusive — lucky sighting
🐃

Buffalo

Deceptively dangerous — considered the most unpredictable of the Big Five. Massive herds of thousands graze the Mara plains.

Common sighting
🦏

Rhino

The rarest of the five. Critically endangered. Any sighting in the Mara conservancies is a genuine privilege — fingers crossed.

Rare — very lucky sighting
